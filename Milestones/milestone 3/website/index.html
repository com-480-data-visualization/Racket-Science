Why Serving Matters?

Serving is one of the most decisive skills in tennis. The greatest servers combine power, accuracy, and nerves of steel, often turning matches in their favor with just a few key points.

To identify the legends of serving, we analyzed key stats: Ace Rate, First Serve %, First Serve Win %, Second Serve Win %, and Break Point Save %. By comparing these metrics, we highlight not just the flashy servers, but those who consistently dominated with their serve and made it a cornerstone of their success.

Why Net Play Matters?

Net play can be a real game-changer in tennis, demanding quick instincts and great tactics. We analyzed four key categories to capture all the ways players use the net:

  • Net Points: Finishing points at the net, showing overall net effectiveness.
  • Approach: Moving forward after an approach shot, highlighting aggressive tactics.
  • Net Points Rallies: Rallies where a player comes to the net during the point, reflecting dynamic play.
  • Approach Rallies: Rallies starting with or involving an approach shot, emphasizing tactical net use.

For each, you can look at Net Efficiency (the % of net points won) or Passing Shots (how well a player handles being attacked at the net). Stats are available overall or year by year, revealing both all-time skill and progression over time.
